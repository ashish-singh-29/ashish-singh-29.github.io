First, let's look at the definition of data-driven decision-making: data-driven decision-making is the process of collecting information from sources outside your control.

The goal of this process is to find out what are the most important things that matter in your life and to make those things more relevant. It is also about finding out what is the best way of doing a particular thing for example, if you want to buy a new car or go on vacation with your family, you need to know what kind of car would fit your budget and if you can take a vacation right now. The more clarity you have in relation to your end goal or objective, the more focused and rewarding your analysis will be.
